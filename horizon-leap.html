The moment when
insight becomes
identity.

Most people know who they want to be. They can describe their Horizon Self — the version of themselves living from their values, contributing what they're here to contribute, inhabiting their life fully.

The gap isn't knowledge. It's embodiment. The distance between understanding something and living from it as if it's already true.

Horizon Leap closes that gap.

This is not incremental progress. It is a discontinuous shift — from someone who knows what they want to be, to someone who is already being it.

What Horizon Leap is.

Horizon Leap is a guided crossing. It takes you from your current honest assessment — where you actually are, across all seven domains — and builds a lived experience of who you are at your Horizon.

Not visualisation. Not affirmations. Not goal-setting. A structured immersion in the life of the person your Horizon Goals are pointing you toward — until that person stops feeling like a future possibility and starts feeling like who you already are.

The shift, when it happens, is not subtle. It reorganises how you think, how you carry yourself, and what feels natural to do next.

The four components.

Component One
Horizon Life

A day in the life of your Horizon Self — in full, sensory, present-tense detail. Not a wish list. A lived account of what it actually feels like to be the person your Horizon Goals describe.

Component Two
Horizon Self Vision

Who you are at your Horizon — your values in action, your pattern of contribution, the qualities that define how you show up. The gap between who you are now and who that person is, seen clearly and without collapse.

Component Three
Horizon Biography

Your origin story, reauthored from the perspective of your Horizon Self. The through-line that makes everything that has happened — including what you wish hadn't — coherent, purposeful, and yours.

Component Four
Target Goals

SMART goals anchored in your Horizon Self — three focus areas, a 90-day sprint, and the specific actions that close the distance between who you are and who you are becoming.

Who this is for.

  • Those who have done the orientation work and have an honest map — but aren't moving from it
  • Those who know exactly who they want to be and keep reverting to who they've always been
  • Those who have insight without identity — understanding without embodiment
  • Those ready for a discontinuous shift, not incremental improvement
  • Those whose Horizon Self is clear enough to step into

Where this fits.

Horizon Leap follows Orienteering. You need an honest map before you can leap from it. If you haven't done Orienteering yet, that's the place to start — it produces the Horizon Goals that Horizon Leap works from.

After Horizon Leap, Pulse is the practice that keeps you living from what you've crossed into. The leap is a moment. The practice is what makes it permanent.
