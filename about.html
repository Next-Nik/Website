NextUs · Life OS

Nik Wood

Founder, Life OS & NextUs · Mexico City

Seven years of silence isn't a gap to explain away — it's proof that the work is real. I didn't rebrand. I didn't pivot for market reasons. I went through the very thing this ecosystem is designed to help people navigate: a skull fracture, cancer, the loss of my mother, and the loss of the friend who saved my life when I broke my skull. What came out the other side isn't a new product line. It's a fundamentally different understanding of what this work is for — and the scale it needs to operate at.

Where it started.

My mother was a naturopathic doctor and a shaman. She surrounded me with healers, teachers, and practitioners — people who understood that the work of becoming a full human being was serious, worth doing, and worth doing well. That world shaped how I see everything.

There were challenges during my youth. Abuse and bullying. Experiences I knew I needed to move through — not around, not past, but through — and do it in a way that left me stronger on the other side.

Jim Rohn's line became an operating principle: you are the average of the five people you spend the most time with. I took that seriously. I read everything. I took every course I could find. I started training formally while I was still a teenager — NLP, hypnotherapy, Timeline Therapy — while most of my peers were still figuring out what they wanted to do with their lives.

I was trying to understand how transformation actually worked — not as a concept, but as a lived process. I've been doing this work ever since. I'm forty-seven now.

Eventually, people started noticing a shift in me. They wanted that shift for themselves. They asked if they could hire me to help them get there. That's how the coaching started.

What the work is.

I help people see who they really are.

I hold a frame that keeps that vision upright until I'm not needed anymore — until my clients can hold it themselves, and fly. I see possibility and speak it into existence. I protect and advocate for someone's Horizon Self — the person they're becoming — until they're aligned and doing that work on their own.

That's harder to name than it sounds. It doesn't fit neatly into the category of coaching. It's not therapy. It's not consulting. It's a particular kind of witnessing — the ability to see what's already there before the person themselves can hold it steadily, and to stay present with that vision across time.

What that looks like in practice.

Years ago I was working with a client who was also a friend — Ben. He'd been running a business for a year and a half. It had flatlined. Barely paying the bills, causing him real stress. Through our work together, we found what was underneath it.

Someone had put a thought into his mind that people who ran social enterprises were fake — that they were taking advantage of the good nature of humans. Somewhere, his subconscious had decided that if he were to succeed running a social enterprise, he'd be one of those people. And he wasn't okay with that.

Once he could see that belief clearly — once he could see that he could run his business with honor and integrity — something unlocked. He went from ,000 a month to 0,000 the following month. Then 00,000 the month after that. The business kept building.

We also worked on what I call the Inner Game. Through that work he met his now-fiancée, built his dream house, and had many incredible adventures. Ben has been on the podcast twice. He tells the story better than I do.

That kind of result isn't about me. It's about what becomes possible when someone can finally see themselves clearly and stop working against their own capacity.

The years in between.

The Life Athletics Podcast launched in 2015. For five years I had conversations with people doing extraordinary work — building lives, building businesses, building themselves. The show grew. The work deepened.

Then the threshold years came. A skull fracture. Cancer. The death of my mother. The death of the friend who had saved my life when I broke my skull. These weren't separate events — they were a sequence that dismantled the life I had built and required me to navigate, genuinely, the very terrain I had been helping others find their way through.

I've spoken publicly about the skull fracture — Episode 197 of the podcast is called Near Death & Rebirth. The rest I've held more quietly. I'm naming them here not because they need to be disclosed, but because they need to be understood as the context for everything that came after.

What came out of those years wasn't a return to coaching. It was an architecture.

What the work became.

Out of those years came a framework for navigating a life — and eventually, for navigating the future we're building together.

Life OS is the personal navigation infrastructure — mapping the seven domains of life (Path, Spark, Body, Finances, Relationships, Inner Game, Outer Game) so you can see where you actually are and move from clarity. Purpose Piece surfaces the pattern beneath how someone is naturally built to contribute. Horizon Path is the facilitated crossing for people ready to change not just what they do, but who they are. NextMen is the men's container. Pulse is the ongoing practice that keeps the map current over time.

NextUs is the civilizational layer — a coordination platform for the people working across the seven domains of the future. The same navigation physics that apply to one life apply at collective scale. That's not a metaphor. It's structural.

The podcast relaunched in 2024 as NextUs — 222 episodes across ten years, two eras, one continuous inquiry.

The vision.

I'm living into a world where everyone is firmly on their path and actively levelling up toward their full-yes life — a world where the godspark of humanity is awakened and amplified.

Everyone has a connection to the Divine. I call it the godspark. That spark — the animating divine fragment in every living thing — is what all of this is in service of. Living into a world where everyone is linked to that internal godspark, fully unlocked, tapped into their innate greatness: that's my Horizon. That's where I'm pointed. That's where I'm heading.

What would be possible from that place is beyond my comprehension.

A life worth living, a future worth building.
